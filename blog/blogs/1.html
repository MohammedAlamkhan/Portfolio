RCS: The New Spam Highway

The Promise vs. Reality

When Google championed RCS as the future of messaging, they promised an enhanced communication experience. Instead, they've delivered what feels like a supercharged spam channel. My messaging inbox has become a wasteland of promotional content, with personal messages drowning in a sea of corporate noise.

Daily Reality Check: This morning alone, I received 7 promotional RCS messages from businesses I've never interacted with, compared to just 3 personal messages from actual contacts.

The Business Message Invasion

Google's "verified business messaging" has become a nightmare. Here's what's really happening:

  • Businesses are exploiting RCS's rich media features to send what amounts to full-screen ads directly to our message inboxes
  • The "verified" status is being handed out too liberally, with minimal vetting of business messaging practices
  • "Suggested replies" are nothing more than clickbait buttons designed to engage users in promotional conversations
  • There's no effective way to bulk-block promotional messages while keeping personal RCS features enabled

The Verification Facade

Google's verification system is a paper tiger. While they claim it prevents spam, the reality is different:

  • Verified businesses can still send unsolicited promotional content
  • The verification process focuses on business legitimacy, not messaging practices
  • There's no meaningful limit on message frequency or content type
  • User consent mechanisms are weak and often buried in fine print

The Dark Patterns

RCS business messages employ manipulative tactics:

  • Auto-expanding rich cards that dominate your screen
  • Deceptive "urgent" notifications that are just sales pitches
  • Persistent notification badges that won't clear until you interact with the message
  • Confusing opt-out processes that often lead to more promotional content

The Missing Controls

What users desperately need but RCS fails to provide:

  • A separate tab for business messages, completely isolated from personal communications
  • One-click permanent blocking of all promotional content
  • Clear, universal opt-out mechanisms that actually work
  • Granular controls over what types of business messages can reach you

The Way Forward

Until Google addresses these issues, I've found these workarounds:

  • Disable RCS completely and use alternative messaging apps for personal communications
  • Use a secondary phone number for business contacts to isolate promotional messages
  • Enable SMS-only mode when possible, sacrificing features for peace of mind

The Bottom Line: RCS has become a spam-filled mess that prioritizes business interests over user experience. Until Google implements serious changes to message control and promotional content limitations, it's failing at its primary purpose: being a better way to communicate with people who matter to us.
